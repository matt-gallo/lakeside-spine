General Chiropractic Care

Common questions about chiropractic treatment

What is chiropractic care?
+

Chiropractic care is a healthcare discipline that focuses on diagnosing and treating neuromuscular disorders, with a special emphasis on treating these conditions through manual adjustment and manipulation of the spine. At Lakeside Spine & Injury Center, Dr. Allen uses precise, hands-on techniques to restore proper joint motion, reduce pain, and improve your body's natural healing ability.

Unlike traditional medicine that often relies on medications to mask symptoms, chiropractic care addresses the root cause of your pain and dysfunction, helping your body heal naturally without drugs or surgery.

Is chiropractic care safe?
+

Yes, chiropractic care is extremely safe when performed by a licensed professional. Dr. Allen has over 23 years of experience and uses evidence-based techniques that are among the safest treatments available for spine-related conditions.

Serious complications from chiropractic adjustments are exceptionally rare, occurring in less than 1 in 2 million treatments. Most patients experience immediate relief with no adverse effects.

What makes the "popping" sound during adjustments?
+

The popping sound you hear during a chiropractic adjustment is caused by the release of nitrogen gas bubbles from the synovial fluid in your joints. This phenomenon is called cavitation and is completely harmless—similar to cracking your knuckles.

The sound doesn't indicate the effectiveness of the treatment. Some adjustments are silent yet equally beneficial for restoring proper joint function and reducing pain.

Do I need a referral to see a chiropractor?
+

No referral is needed to see Dr. Allen at Lakeside Spine & Injury Center. As a primary healthcare provider, you can schedule directly with our office. This allows you to get the care you need without delays from other healthcare providers.

However, some insurance plans may require a referral for coverage, so it's worth checking your specific plan details if you're using insurance.

Can children receive chiropractic care?
+

Absolutely! Children can safely benefit from gentle chiropractic care. Kids are naturally active and may experience falls, sports injuries, or postural issues from heavy backpacks and prolonged sitting.

Dr. Allen uses age-appropriate techniques that are much gentler than adult treatments. Many parents bring their children for wellness care to support proper growth and development, improve posture, and maintain optimal spinal health.

Will I have to keep coming back forever?
+

Not at all. Dr. Allen's goal is to get you better as quickly as possible and teach you how to maintain your improvement. The number of visits depends on your specific condition, how long you've had the problem, and your overall health.

Some patients feel better after just a few visits, while others with chronic conditions may need ongoing care. You're always in control of your treatment plan, and there are never high-pressure sales tactics or long-term contracts at our cash-based practice.

Treatments & Techniques

Understanding your treatment options

What treatment techniques do you use?
+

At Lakeside Spine & Injury Center, we offer a comprehensive range of evidence-based treatments including spinal manipulation, soft-tissue therapy with IASTM tools, dry needling, cupping therapy, posture analysis, and therapeutic exercise programs.

Dr. Allen customizes your treatment plan based on your specific condition, preferences, and goals. This integrated approach addresses not just your symptoms, but the underlying cause of your pain.

What is dry needling and how does it differ from acupuncture?
+

Dry needling is a technique that uses thin needles to target muscle trigger points and release tension. Unlike acupuncture, which is based on traditional Chinese medicine and energy meridians, dry needling is grounded in Western medicine and focuses on neuromuscular dysfunction.

Dr. Allen uses dry needling to treat specific muscle knots and fascial restrictions that don't respond well to other treatments. Many patients experience immediate relief from chronic muscle tension and improved range of motion.

Does dry needling hurt?
+

Most patients describe dry needling as minimally uncomfortable. You might feel a brief, mild sting when the needle is inserted, similar to a mosquito bite. When the needle hits a trigger point, you may feel a muscle twitch or cramping sensation that lasts just a few seconds.

Many patients are surprised by how tolerable the treatment is and often feel immediate relief as muscle tension releases during the session.

What is IASTM (Instrument-Assisted Soft Tissue Mobilization)?
+

IASTM is a technique that uses specially designed stainless steel tools to detect and treat areas of soft tissue fibrosis or chronic inflammation. These FDA-approved tools allow Dr. Allen to precisely target scar tissue and adhesions that limit mobility and cause pain.

The treatment creates controlled micro-trauma that stimulates your body's natural healing response, breaking down scar tissue and promoting the formation of healthy, functional tissue.

How does cupping therapy work?
+

Cupping uses glass or silicone cups to create suction on the skin, lifting the underlying fascia and improving circulation. This increased blood flow helps reduce muscle tension, promotes healing, and flushes metabolic waste from tissues.

The treatment feels like a deep, stretching sensation and is quite relaxing for most patients. Cupping integrates perfectly with chiropractic adjustments and other therapies to enhance your overall treatment results.

Will adjustments hurt?
+

Chiropractic adjustments are generally not painful. Most patients experience immediate relief and describe the sensation as satisfying, like "scratching an itch you couldn't reach." Dr. Allen uses gentle, precise techniques tailored to your comfort level.

Some patients may experience mild soreness similar to post-exercise muscle fatigue for 24-48 hours after treatment, which is completely normal as your body adjusts to improved alignment.

Auto Accident Injuries

Recovery and treatment after car accidents

Should I see a chiropractor after a car accident?
+

Yes, it's highly recommended to see a chiropractor after any car accident, even if you feel fine initially. Many auto accident injuries like whiplash don't show symptoms immediately due to adrenaline and shock masking pain.

Early chiropractic intervention can prevent minor injuries from becoming chronic problems and help document your injuries for insurance purposes. Dr. Allen offers same-day appointments for accident victims.

What is whiplash and how do you treat it?
+

Whiplash occurs when your head is suddenly jerked backward and forward during an accident, causing injury to the cervical spine, muscles, and ligaments. Symptoms can include neck pain, headaches, dizziness, shoulder pain, and cognitive issues.

Dr. Allen treats whiplash with gentle spinal adjustments, soft tissue therapy, dry needling for muscle spasms, and rehabilitation exercises to restore normal neck function and prevent chronic pain.

How long does it take to recover from whiplash?
+

Recovery time varies depending on the severity of injury and how quickly treatment begins. Mild cases may resolve in 2-4 weeks, while more severe injuries can take several months. Early intervention typically leads to faster, more complete recovery.

Factors affecting recovery include age, overall health, previous injuries, and compliance with treatment recommendations. Dr. Allen provides realistic timelines based on your specific case.

Will my auto insurance cover chiropractic treatment?
+

Most auto insurance policies include Personal Injury Protection (PIP) or Medical Payments (MedPay) coverage that covers chiropractic care after an accident. We can help you navigate your insurance benefits and work directly with your insurance company.

Our cash-based model also means transparent pricing with no surprise bills or complicated insurance hassles if you prefer to pay directly.
